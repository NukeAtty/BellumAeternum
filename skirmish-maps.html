Leavenworth

Nestled at the foot of the Alps, this mountain town was once all but forgotten by the world. During World War II, it was temporarily taken over by German forces as a logistical supply point, where railways and highways were built. After the war, the town regained its former tranquility. However, a different war has now transformed this forgotten town into a strategic point once again.

Mt.Thunderhead

Connected to the Alps, it was originally a village that attracted little attention. After the war broke out, strange thunderstorms began appearing frequently over its mountaintops—lightning clearly visible even at its base. Many locals claimed this phenomenon only started after Allied forces entered the area, though such claims were swiftly dismissed by officials. Today, it has become an ideal campsite, with residents from surrounding towns driving here to witness the spectacle.

Sturmburg

Since the Middle Ages, the lord of this secluded mountain village has held sway over the land. Yet, the war's outbreak and its resulting scarcity of resources have inflamed tensions, with various powers now plotting to seize control. Even the lord of Sturmburg is now reaching out to outside forces, preparing to defend the legacy painstakingly built over generations.

Modern Times

As the two global superpowers, the United States and the Soviet Union, confronted each other, they began to transfer their surplus basic industries to Europe. In a short period of time, industrial zones have spread through almost all the towns in European continent. With the outbreak of war, these industrial zones quickly became fiercely contested territories, and the labours continue to work amidst the conflict just to survive.
